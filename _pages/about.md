My Name is Sebastian Koch, I'm a Master's student in Computer Science at the [University of Tübingen](https://uni-tuebingen.de/). My main focus during my Master's degree lies in the area of machine learning, computer vision, and robotics. 

Currently, I'm working on my Master-Thesis at the [Bosch Center for Artificial Intelligence](https://www.bosch-ai.com/) on the topic of Multi-View RGB-D Fusion for 6D Pose Estimation.
Additionally, I'm a research assistant at the [Cognitive Systems Lab of Professor Zell](https://uni-tuebingen.de/fakultaeten/mathematisch-naturwissenschaftliche-fakultaet/fachbereiche/informatik/lehrstuehle/kognitive-systeme/) where I work closely with other Ph.D. students on papers and novel ideas for object detection. 

Before studying at the University of Tübingen I was doing my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science at the [Baden-Württemberg Cooperative State University Stuttgart](https://www.dhbw-stuttgart.de/en/about-us/) in cooperation with the [Bosch](https://www.bosch.com/). The university focuses on a very practical approach providing the opportunity to work at a partnering company for several months during the semester. At Bosch, I worked on various projects related to autonomous driving, robotics and machine learning. Gaining a lot of practical knowledge next to the theoretical knowledge gained at the university.
Through my outstanding work at Bosch, I was accepted into the [Students@Bosch](https://www.bosch.de/karriere/dein-einstieg/studentinnen-und-studenten/studentsbosch/) program which supports students in their future academic career.
