Hi I'm Sebastian, a PhD Student at [Bosch Research](https://www.bosch.com/research/). I am co-supervised by [Prof. Timo Ropinski](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=FuY-lbcAAAAJ&hl=en) from the [University Ulm](https://www.uni-ulm.de/) and [Mirco Colosi](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=k4m1c6EAAAAJ&hl=en) from Bosch Research. My goal of my PhD is to develop visual scene representations relevant for several downstream tasks in mobile robotics. Therefore, my research interest lies at the intersection of machine learning, computer vision, and robotics. 

Prior to my PhD I was a master student at the [University of Tübingen](https://uni-tuebingen.de/), where I mainly focused on varying courses on fundamental machine learning, computer vision and robotics. For my master thesis I focused on Multi-View RGB-D Fusion for 6D Pose Estimation co-supervised by [Prof. Andreas Geiger](http://www.cvlibs.net/) and [Prof. Gerhard Neumann](https://alr.anthropomatik.kit.edu/21_65.php) and in cooperation with the [Bosch Center for Artificial Intelligence (BCAI)](https://www.bosch-ai.com/).
Additionally, during my time as a master student, I worked as a research assistant at the [Cognitive Systems Lab of Prof. Andreas Zell](https://uni-tuebingen.de/fakultaeten/mathematisch-naturwissenschaftliche-fakultaet/fachbereiche/informatik/lehrstuehle/kognitive-systeme/) where I worked closely with other Ph.D. students on papers and novel ideas for object detection. Resulting in a paper about camera parameters for object detection models [(arxiv)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.00306).

Before studying at the University of Tübingen I was doing my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science at the [Baden-Württemberg Cooperative State University Stuttgart](https://www.dhbw-stuttgart.de/en/about-us/) in cooperation with the [Bosch](https://www.bosch.com/). The university focuses on a very practical approach providing the opportunity to work at a partnering company for several months during the semester. At Bosch, I worked on various projects related to autonomous driving, robotics and machine learning. Gaining a lot of practical knowledge next to the theoretical knowledge gained at the university.
Through my outstanding work at Bosch, I was accepted into the [Students@Bosch](https://www.bosch.de/karriere/dein-einstieg/studentinnen-und-studenten/studentsbosch/) program which supports students in their future academic career.
